The Sisters of Mary are a Roman Catholic religious congregation founded in 1964 by Venerable Aloysius Schwartz in Busan, South Korea. They dedicate their lives to serving the poorest of the poor, especially orphans and abandoned children, through "Boystowns" and "Girlstowns" that provide free education, housing, and formation in many countries.

Founder

  • Venerable Aloysius Schwartz
  • September 18, 1930, in Washington, D.C.
  • August 15, 1964 – established the Sisters of Mary in Busan to create "Boystowns" and "Girlstowns."
  • Inspired by the Virgin of Banneux (the "Benefactress of the Poor").

Goal

The congregation's main goal is to help poor and abandoned children break the cycle of poverty by giving them free education, food, shelter, and moral‑spiritual formation so they can become productive, responsible, and faith‑filled members of society.

Mission

The mission of the Sisters of Mary is to prepare poor children for a better future in their own country at no cost to their families, providing full‑time care, academic formation, and Christian values through their "Boystowns" and "Girlstowns."

Philosophy

Their educational philosophy is rooted in integral Christian formation, guided by the pillars of Study, Play, Work, and Pray, and inspired by the example of Mary and the teachings of Jesus. The Sisters seek to form the whole person—body, mind, and spirit—so that students live with humility, joy, and a sense of mission.

Girls' Town and Boys' Town Locations

  • Girlstown Biga, Barangay Biga, Silang, Cavite
  • Boystown Adlas, Barangay Adlas, Silang, Cavite
  • Girlstown Talisay, J.P. Rizal Street, Talisay City, Cebu
  • Boystown Minglanilla, Tungkop, Minglanilla, Cebu

Outside the Philippines, the Sisters also run Girlstown and Boystown programs in Mexico (for example, a Girls' Town in Chalco and a Boys' Town in Guadalajara) and similar communities in parts of Africa and Asia, following the same model.
